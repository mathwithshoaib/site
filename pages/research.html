Research

Doctoral Research

Pneumonia–Influenza Co-Infection Model

My doctoral research (under Dr. Adnan Khan at LUMS) develops and analyses a multi-compartment ODE system for co-infection dynamics — studying how two infectious diseases interact simultaneously within a host population. The model has 11 compartments tracking individuals through susceptible, exposed, singly infected, co-infected, and recovered states.

Key Questions

  • → Computing ℛ₀ via Next Generation Matrix
  • → Stability of the disease-free equilibrium
  • → Existence of endemic equilibria
  • → Backward bifurcation analysis
  • → Sensitivity analysis of ℛ₀
