Calculus I

The language in which physics, economics, and engineering are written. Taught in two parallel tracks — rigorously from first principles, and through the lens of business and economic application.

Textbooks
Stewart · Hoffmann
Track A

Rigorous Track

Epsilon-delta definitions, formal proofs, classical analysis. Based on Stewart's Calculus.

Track B

Applied Track

Business, economics & social science applications. Based on Hoffmann's Calculus.

Topics Covered

Limits & Continuity

The foundation. What does it mean for a function to approach a value? Formal definitions, limit laws, continuity.

Differentiation

Rates of change, tangent lines, all differentiation rules, implicit differentiation, and applications.

Integration

Antiderivatives, Riemann sums, the Fundamental Theorem, and techniques of integration.

Differential Equations

Introduction to separable DEs, initial value problems, and continuous compounding.

Prerequisites: Pre-Calculus or equivalent. Familiarity with functions, algebra, and basic trigonometry.
