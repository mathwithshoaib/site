Surprising, delightful, and mind-bending mathematical experiences. Interact, discover, and wonder.

Interactive Activity

🌙 To the Moon — Paper Folding

How many times do you need to fold a piece of paper to reach the Moon? The answer will shock you — and it perfectly illustrates the explosive power of exponential growth. Drag the slider and watch the thickness skyrocket past buildings, mountains, and into space.

🚀

🔺 Sierpiński Triangle — Chaos Game

Click "Generate" to watch a fractal emerge from pure randomness. Start with three points, pick one at random, move halfway towards it, plot a dot — repeat 10,000 times. Order from chaos.

🌀 Ulam Spiral — Primes Hide in Patterns

In 1963, Stanisław Ulam discovered that primes, when arranged in a spiral, form striking diagonal patterns. Nobody fully understands why. Click to generate the spiral and see the mystery for yourself.
